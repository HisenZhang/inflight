# Quick Start Guide

Get up and running with InFlight in 4 simple steps!

## Step 1: Load Database

Before planning any routes, you need to load the aviation database.

1. Click the **DATA** tab
2. Click **LOAD DATABASE** button
3. Wait for the download to complete (may take 30-60 seconds)
4. You'll see database statistics when complete

> ðŸ’¡ **Tip**: The database is cached locally, so you only need to do this once unless you clear your browser data.

## Step 2: Enter Your Route

Now you're ready to plan a flight!

1. Click the **ROUTE** tab
2. Enter your **DEPARTURE** airport (e.g., `KALB`)
3. Enter your **ROUTE** (optional - leave blank for direct):
   - Example: `PAYGE Q822 FNT WYNDE3`
   - Supports airways, SIDs, STARs, navaids, and fixes
4. Enter your **DESTINATION** airport (e.g., `KORD`)
5. *(Optional)* Enable **WIND CORRECTION & TIME**:
   - Enter cruise altitude and true airspeed
   - Select forecast period (6/12/24 hours)
6. *(Optional)* Enable **FUEL PLANNING**:
   - Enter fuel capacity, burn rate, and reserves
7. Click **CALCULATE**

## Step 3: Review Your Navlog

The navigation log shows your complete flight plan.

1. Click the **NAVLOG** tab
2. Review waypoints and leg information:
   - Waypoint codes (color-coded by type)
   - Headings and distances
   - Wind correction angles (if enabled)
   - Fuel calculations (if enabled)
3. Click airport codes to view on AirNav.com
4. *(Optional)* Click **EXPORT** to save your navlog

> ðŸŽ¯ **Waypoint Colors**:
> - **Cyan** = Airports
> - **Magenta** = Navaids
> - **White** = Fixes
> - **Amber** = Reporting Points

## Step 4: Use the Moving Map

Track your position in real-time during the flight.

1. Click the **MAP** tab
2. View the route visualization
3. Click **Enable GPS** (browser will request location permission)
4. Use zoom controls:
   - **ROUTE** - Show entire route
   - **DEST** - Zoom to destination
   - **50NM/25NM** - Tactical view around aircraft
   - **+/âˆ’** - Manual zoom
5. Navigation panel shows:
   - Next waypoint
   - Required heading
   - Distance and ETE
   - Ground speed and GPS accuracy

> ðŸ“± **Mobile Features**:
> - Device vibrates when switching waypoints (within 2nm)
> - Audio announcements with waypoint and heading information

## Optional: Track Your Flight

Record your flight for later analysis.

1. Click the **STATS** tab
2. GPS tracking starts automatically when airborne
3. View real-time statistics:
   - Flight time
   - Fuel remaining (if fuel planning enabled)
   - GPS accuracy
4. Click **EXPORT** to download GPX track
5. Tracks are saved in the **DATA** tab
